Dear Friend,
There’s a lie so old and so pervasive that we barely notice it anymore: You are not enough. It’s in a parent’s unmet expectations. A teacher’s careless comments. Comparisons with people we’ll never meet. Once planted, the lie takes root, growing bigger everyday. And when it grows, it hurts. Because no matter what we achieve, who we love, or what we buy, nothing on the outside can fix a wound that started on the inside.
I know this because I lived it.
I built TOMS, a company that gave away over 100 million pairs of shoes to children in need. I had the dream house, the vacations, the money, the marriage, the friends.
And yet, none of it touched the pain I carried — the fear that I was never enough. The depression that followed nearly made me take my own life 18 months ago.
The truth I discovered in that moment — the truth that saved me — is simple: I have always been enough. And so have you. Today, I am dedicated to spending the rest of my life spreading this message and creating a cultural intervention that is desperately needed. This can only happen with powerful voices like yourself speaking up as well.
I am enough,
You are enough,
We are enough.
